# **Z# Project Code of Conduct**

## **1. Purpose**

The Z# project aims to foster an open, collaborative, and welcoming environment for contributors of all backgrounds and skill levels. This Code of Conduct establishes expectations for behavior and outlines processes for reporting and addressing unacceptable conduct.

## **2. Expected Behavior**

All participants in the Z# community—contributors, maintainers, users, and observers—are expected to:

* Treat all individuals with respect and professionalism.
* Communicate constructively and in good faith.
* Provide clear, actionable feedback.
* Welcome differing viewpoints and technical perspectives.
* Support a collaborative environment where learning and growth are encouraged.
* Follow project guidelines, including coding standards, contribution rules, and documentation expectations.

## **3. Unacceptable Behavior**

The following conduct is prohibited within the Z# community and its associated spaces:

* Harassment, discrimination, or derogatory remarks targeting individuals or groups.
* Threats, intimidation, or other behavior that compromises the safety of participants.
* Persistent disruptive behavior, including excessive hostility, unproductive argumentation, or refusal to accept reasonable boundaries.
* Sharing private information without consent.
* Any attempt to misuse project infrastructure, exploit vulnerabilities, or distribute malicious code.

## **4. Scope**

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces associated with the Z# project, including:

* GitHub repositories and issue trackers
* Documentation and websites
* Chat platforms
* Mailing lists
* Any in-person or virtual events officially connected to the project

## **5. Reporting Violations**

Concerns, violations, or questions may be reported to the project maintainers through the designated channels (e.g., official email, GitHub reporting tools). Reports will be handled promptly, respectfully, and confidentially.

## **6. Enforcement**

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct. They may take any action deemed appropriate, including but not limited to:

* Warning the offending party
* Temporarily or permanently banning individuals from project spaces
* Reverting or blocking harmful contributions
* Escalating issues to higher authorities when necessary

All enforcement decisions will consider the nature and severity of the conduct, as well as the safety and well-being of the community.

## **7. Attribution**

This Code of Conduct is adapted from widely recognized open-source community guidelines and may be updated as the project evolves.
